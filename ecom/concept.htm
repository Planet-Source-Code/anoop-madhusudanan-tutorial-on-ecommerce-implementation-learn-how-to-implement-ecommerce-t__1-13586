2. ECOMMERCE TRANSACTIONS
A full coverage of ECommerce Transactions is out of the scope of this article. But of course, I am going to discuss the concepts of card-based transactions, in this section.

Payment card organizations worldwide have established global networks and rules (protocols) for these networks, that allow people who own cards issued by one bank to use those cards at almost any merchant location worldwide.

To understand things better, let us see how a Card Brand's network functions. A Card brand network normally uses the services provided by:

1. Issuers—An Issuer is normally a bank system, that issues cards to cardholders.

2. Acquirers—An Acquirer is also a bank (in most cases), that signs a contract with a merchant. Once a contract is established, the merchant can accept credit cards as a payment method.

3. PAYMENT PROCESS

Before going any step further, kindly understand the meaning of the following terms (Feeling very basic? But it is important)

  • Issuer - A bank that issues cards to cardholders

  • Acquirer - A bank, that signs a contract with a merchant, for helping the merchant to accept card payments.

  • Merchant - An individual or an organization, with a Merchant Account (an account provided by that merchant's acquirer), for accepting credit card payments.

  • Cardholder - An individual with a credit card in his hand (that he may use to buy products)

Now let us analyze a Credit Card-based payment process. A basic payment process consists of the following steps. (The picture may not look great, because I drawn than very fast - in Word - But I hope you can understand it. :-) )

1. A transaction is authorized at the point in time when the purchase takes place.

Authorization refers to the process by which the Acquirer receives the transaction (from the Merchant) in real time and sends the transaction details to the Issuer through the card brand payment network. It consists of three main steps. 

  1. The Issuer checks that the card is valid and that the cardholder has adequate credit available to cover the amount of the transaction. 

  2. The Issuer then informs the Acquirer of the result of the authorization request by sending a response message back to the Acquirer over the brand network.  

  3. The Acquirer then informs the Merchant (who is waiting for a response) in real time of the Issuer’s decision.

2. The transaction is Submitted for Settlement at the time when the goods are physically dispatched (by the merchant to the cardholder).

A Merchant may send the details of the transaction to the Acquirer, typically in a file or “batch” with all the other transactions that are to be settled on that particular day. This is termed as Submission For Settlement. It mainly consists of three simple steps.

  1. The Merchant sends the details to the Acquirer.

  2. In most cases, the Acquirer pays the Merchant and sends a request for payment to the Issuer.

  3. The Issuer subsequently pays the Acquirer through the brand network and bills the Cardholder.

Of course, to implement ECommerce in your site, you may not need to perform all these transactions yourself. A number of 3rd party services, to simplify the transactions between a merchant and an acquiring system, are available. We will see more details in next chapter.
